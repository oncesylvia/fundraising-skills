# Investor diligence checklist

Work through these before reaching out or taking a meeting. Mark each
`verified` / `couldn't verify`. Cite a source for every "yes."

## Fit

- [ ] **Stage** — do they invest at my stage, and is their check size right for
      my round? (A follower won't fill a lead-shaped hole.)
- [ ] **Sector** — explicit thesis or, better, *recent* portfolio evidence in my
      exact space (not just the broad label).
- [ ] **Geography / jurisdiction** — can they invest in my location and entity
      structure?
- [ ] **Ownership target** — do they need a % that my round can give them?

## Activity (are they actually deploying?)

- [ ] Led or co-led a deal in the last ~6–12 months?
- [ ] Any new investments in my space recently?
- [ ] Is the current fund early enough in its life to be making *new* bets (vs.
      reserving for follow-ons)? A late-in-life fund deploys differently.

## The partner

- [ ] Identified the **specific partner** to target (not just the firm).
- [ ] They personally focus on / have written about / backed companies in my
      space.
- [ ] They have the authority to champion a deal at this stage.

## Conflicts & risks

- [ ] No **direct competitor** in their portfolio (usually disqualifying — flag
      immediately).
- [ ] No adjacent portfolio company so close it creates an info-leak concern.
- [ ] Any structural terms they're known to require (ROFR, heavy info rights,
      board control at seed)?

## Process & behavior

- [ ] How fast do they typically move? (Sets your expectations and follow-up.)
- [ ] Do they lead or only follow at this stage?
- [ ] Reserve / follow-on posture — will they support the next round?
- [ ] Board involvement and style.
- [ ] Founder-friendliness signals (sourced founder references, their writing,
      downturn behavior). Record green and red flags fairly.

## Contact

- [ ] Preferred contact route (warm intro / submit form / public email).
- [ ] Is there a likely warm path? (If yes, route to the `warm-intro` skill.)

## Questions to ask them (diligence them back)

Pick 4–6 that fit:
- What's your reserve strategy for follow-on rounds?
- How do you typically help between board meetings?
- What does the partnership decision process look like, and on what timeline?
- Where have you been most/least helpful to founders like me?
- What would you need to see to get to conviction?
- Can I talk to one or two founders you've backed (including one that didn't go
  well)?
