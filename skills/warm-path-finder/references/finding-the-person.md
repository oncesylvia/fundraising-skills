# Finding the right person at a firm

Pitching "the firm" is a mistake — you pitch a person. Investing firms route
deals by sector and stage to specific partners, and the human who *champions*
your deal internally is the one to reach. This is about identifying that person
and their public footprint from legitimate sources — not harvesting contacts.

## Who you're actually looking for

At most firms the decision-makers form a ladder; target by role + your sector:

- **General / Managing Partner (GP)** — decides and leads. Famous, hard to reach
  cold, usually needs a warm intro. Right target at later stages or when they
  personally own your thesis.
- **Partner / Principal** — often owns a sector vertical; can champion a deal.
  Frequently the sweet spot: senior enough to push, reachable enough to engage.
- **Senior associate / senior investment manager / VP** — **sources** deals and
  is actively *looking* for founders. Often the **best first contact**: more
  responsive, will shepherd you to the partner. Don't overlook them chasing a
  big GP name.
- **Scout / venture partner / EIR** — part-time deal sourcers; a low-friction way
  in, especially at pre-seed.

Rule of thumb: at pre-seed/seed, a principal or senior associate who covers your
space and replies is worth more than a GP who never opens your email.

## How to identify them (public sources)

1. **Firm team page** — list partners and their stated focus areas. Map your
   sector to the person who owns it.
2. **Who led the round for companies like you** — search
   `"<analogous company> <seed/Series A>" <firm> led partner` and read the
   announcement; the named partner has proven appetite for your space and often
   took the board seat. This is the highest-signal match.
3. **Their public thesis** — partners post on X, write firm blog posts, go on
   podcasts. Someone who *wrote about your exact problem* is your champion.
   Capture the specific post/episode — it's your personalization hook later.
4. **Board seats / portfolio** — a partner sitting on boards in your category
   knows the space and the buyers.
5. **Conference talks / panels** — who represents the firm on your topic.

## Capture (research notes, not a contact dump)

For the 1–2 people who fit best, record:

- Name, exact title, the firm.
- **Why them**: the sector/stage fit + the specific evidence (led X's round,
  wrote about Y).
- **Public footprint**: firm bio URL, LinkedIn profile URL, X handle, the most
  relevant post/podcast.
- **Likely contact route**: warm intro (preferred), open X DMs, firm submit
  form, public conference. (How you *reach* them is Step 3–5 of the skill.)
- **Source link + confidence**: `verified` (their own bio/the round
  announcement) vs `likely` (third-party aggregator) vs `unverified — check`.

## Anti-patterns

- Targeting the founder/GP by default because the name is famous — check who
  actually covers your sector and who *sources*.
- Asserting a person's role or focus from memory — confirm on the firm site or a
  round announcement; titles and coverage change.
- Turning this into email-guessing. Identifying the right person ≠ harvesting
  their private inbox. The reach happens via a warm path or a public route.
