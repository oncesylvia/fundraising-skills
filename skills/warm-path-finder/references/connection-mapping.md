# Connection mapping — finding & ranking warm paths

Once you know the person, find who can introduce you. The best warm paths hide in
the founder's **own authorized data**; the rest are found in public. Mine every
surface, then rank by trust × reachability so the founder spends their best
shots wisely.

## Tier 1 — the founder's own authorized data (start here)

Highest hit-rate and 100% legitimate. Ask the founder what they can provide.

### Gmail (if connected as an MCP tool)
Search their history for existing links to the target firm/person:
- Search the **firm name**, the **person's name**, and the firm's **domain** —
  past threads mean a relationship already exists.
- Look at **who else was on threads** with the founder and is now connected to
  the target (shared recipients = a mutual).
- Past intros the founder *received* reveal connectors who intro for a living.

### LinkedIn connections export (the authorized way to "read your LinkedIn")
There is **no API** for a user's connections, and scraping breaks ToS. The legit
route: the founder exports their own data.
- LinkedIn → **Settings → Data Privacy → Get a copy of your data → Connections**
  → download the CSV (name, company, position, and any email they shared).
- Hand the CSV to the skill. Cross-reference against:
  - people **at the target firm**,
  - **portfolio founders** of the firm (the firm's own portfolio list × the
    CSV's companies — these are gold-tier introducers),
  - people sharing the target person's **employer or school**.

### Contacts
Google/phone contacts, same matching. Quick to check, often overlooked.

## Tier 2 — public network surfaces

For paths not in the founder's data:

- **Portfolio-founder paths (best external path)**: a founder the firm already
  backed vouching for you carries the most weight. Identify portfolio companies
  near your space (firm's portfolio page / Crunchbase); see if the founder — or
  a Tier-1 contact — can reach one.
- **X/Twitter**: find the partners/managers, read their public thesis, and judge
  reachability. Many investors keep **open DMs** specifically to source. The
  founder confirms the DM button is present on the profile; the skill drafts a
  tight DM or a thoughtful reply-to-their-post as the opener.
- **Shared communities**: accelerator alumni networks, university/employer
  alumni, angel syndicates, founder Slacks/Discords, mutual advisors, cap-table
  angels who know the firm.
- **Events**: conferences/demo days where the firm speaks — a 2-minute in-person
  hello converts far better than a cold DM.

## Tier 3 — founder does it logged-in (manual)

You can't automate these, but you can tell the founder exactly what to check:
- **LinkedIn "shared connections" / "How you're connected"** on the target's
  profile — who do you both know? (Sales Navigator if they have it.)
- **X DM-open status** — open the profile, look for the message icon.

## The ranking rubric: trust × reachability

Score each path; rank high-trust **and** reachable first.

**Trust** — how much the target trusts the introducer:
| Score | Introducer |
|---|---|
| ★★★ | A founder the firm has **funded**; a close colleague the partner rates |
| ★★ | A respected peer / former coworker / co-investor; a cap-table angel |
| ★ | An acquaintance, a weak tie, a "we met once" |

**Reachability** — how easily the founder can ask, and likelihood of a yes:
| Score | Situation |
|---|---|
| ★★★ | Close relationship; the founder can ask today and expects a yes |
| ★★ | Knows them, needs a careful ask |
| ★ | Barely knows them / would itself need an intro |

**Pick the path that's high on both.** A ★★★-trust introducer the founder can't
actually reach is worthless; a ★★★-reachable acquaintance the target doesn't
trust is weak. The ideal: a portfolio founder the founder genuinely knows.

## Output: a ranked shortlist (not a dump)

| Introducer | Their link to the target | Trust | Reachability | How founder knows them | Source (Tier) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Then: **the single recommended path**, and hand it to `warm-intro`. If the top
rows are all weak, say so honestly and route to `cold-email` / X DM via the
person's public thesis instead — a thin real list beats a padded one.

## Ethics recap

- Only the founder's **own** exported/authorized data + **public** info.
- Never scrape a login-walled graph; never guess/buy private emails.
- Never assert a mutual connection that isn't in the data — no fabricated ties.
