# Sector taxonomy — turn "what we do" into searchable categories

Investors organize by thesis. To find them you must translate the founder's
one-liner into the **categories investors use to describe themselves**, then
search those. Pick the primary category + 1–2 adjacent ones, plus the relevant
modifiers.

## Primary categories (how funds self-describe)

- **B2B SaaS / enterprise software** — vertical SaaS, horizontal tools, dev
  tools, security, data infra, devops
- **AI / ML** — AI infrastructure, AI applications, agents, ML tooling
  (note: most funds now claim AI — qualify on real check + relevant portfolio)
- **Fintech** — payments, banking/BaaS, lending, insurtech, wealth, crypto/web3
- **Consumer** — social, creator, commerce/D2C, subscription, marketplaces
- **Marketplaces / platforms** — two-sided, B2B marketplaces, gig
- **Healthcare / bio** — digital health, healthtech, biotech, medtech
  (biotech/medtech have specialist funds — generalists rarely fit)
- **Deep tech / hard tech** — robotics, semiconductors, space, energy, climate
- **Climate / energy** — climate tech, energy transition, sustainability
- **Hardware** — connected devices, consumer hardware (capital-intensive; needs
  hardware-comfortable investors)
- **Industrial / supply chain / logistics**
- **Gov / defense / regtech**

## Useful modifiers (stack onto the primary)

- **Business model**: SaaS, transactional/take-rate, usage-based, marketplace,
  D2C, open-source/commercial
- **Customer**: SMB, mid-market, enterprise, developer, prosumer, consumer
- **Motion**: PLG (product-led), sales-led, community-led
- **Geography**: US, Europe, LatAm, SEA, India, Africa, MENA, China
- **Special angles**: technical/founder-friendly, diversity-focused, mission/
  impact, solo-GP, operator-led

## Search-query recipe

Combine `primary + modifier + stage` and run several variants:

- `"<primary category> <stage> investors"`
- `"funds investing in <primary> for <customer> 2025"`
- `"<sub-niche> <geo> seed funds"`
- `"who invested in <analogous company> seed/Series A"`
- `"<category> angel investors"` and `"<category> operator angels"`
- `"<sector> accelerator <geo>"` (for pre-seed/idea stage)

## A caution on "AI everything"

Since nearly every fund now markets itself as AI-investing, the label is low
signal. Qualify AI-adjacent startups on: (1) recent *relevant* checks in the
last 12 months, (2) whether their portfolio shows real depth in your exact wedge
(infra vs. application vs. agents are different theses), and (3) check size fit.
Recency and specificity beat the keyword.
