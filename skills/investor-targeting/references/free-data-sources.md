# Free & freemium sources for finding investors

Use these as live search targets. None require a paid subscription for the basic
research this skill does. Always open the source and confirm details before
recommending — listings go stale.

## Investor directories (free / freemium)

- **OpenVC** (openvc.app) — searchable list of funds with stated stage, sector,
  geography, and check size; many include a direct submit/contact route. Strong
  first stop for cold-but-warm outreach because it's opt-in.
- **NFX Signal** (signal.nfx.com) — find investors by stage/sector and see warm
  intro paths; free tier is useful.
- **AngelList / Wellfound** — syndicates and angels, often with public theses.
- **Crunchbase (free tier)** — confirm a fund's recent investments and stage;
  free results are limited but enough to verify "do they invest in my space."
- **Visible.vc Connect, Foundersuite, Shizune, and similar** — periodically
  publish free curated investor lists by stage/sector/geo.
- **VC firm websites directly** — the source of truth for thesis, stage, check
  size, portfolio, and **how to submit a deal**. Always fetch this.

## "Who funded my analogs" (the highest-signal angle)

- **Crunchbase / Tracxn free pages** — look up a non-competing company one step
  ahead of you in your category and read its funding history → those investors
  have proven appetite.
- **SEC EDGAR — Form D filings** (sec.gov/edgar) — US companies disclose
  fundraises here; you can see who's actively writing checks in a space.
- **Press: TechCrunch, Axios Pro Rata, Strictly VC, Term Sheet** — round
  announcements name the lead and participating investors.

## Angels & operators

- **LinkedIn** — search "angel investor" + your category; many operators list
  it. Use connection requests / public posts, not guessed emails.
- **Twitter/X** — many seed investors and angels post their thesis openly;
  search "<category> seed" / "angel investing in <category>".
- **Angel syndicate platforms** (AngelList, Sweater, etc.) — public lead theses.

## Accelerators & studios (pre-seed / idea stage)

- **Y Combinator, Techstars, Antler, AngelPad, sector-specific programs** —
  check focus, geography, batch timing, and whether applications are open.
- Search "<sector> accelerator" and "<region> startup accelerator".

## Newsletters & communities

- Sector and regional VC newsletters often publish "active investors in X" lists.
- Founder communities (On Deck, regional Slacks, alumni networks) surface who's
  actually deploying right now — more current than any static list.

## How to use these without hallucinating

1. Search the directory or press for candidates.
2. Open each candidate's **own site** to confirm stage / sector / check / geo /
   contact route.
3. Record the **source URL** for every entry.
4. Mark confidence: `verified` (confirmed on their site or a filing),
   `likely` (consistent across two third-party sources), `unverified — check`.
