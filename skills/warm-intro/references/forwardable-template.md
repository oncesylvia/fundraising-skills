# Forwardable intro templates

The double opt-in, forwardable intro is the standard. You send your connector a
note + a paste-ready blurb; they check with the investor, then forward the blurb.
Make it effortless.

## A. Note to your connector (the ask)

Keep it warm, specific, and easy to decline.

> Hi <Connector>,
>
> Hope you're well — <one genuine personal line>.
>
> Quick ask: would you be open to introducing me to <Investor Name> at <Fund>?
> We're raising our <stage> round and <Fund> is a strong fit because <one
> specific reason — they led <PortfolioCo>, their thesis on <topic>>. Totally
> understand if it's not a fit for you to make — no pressure at all.
>
> If it's easy, I've written a short blurb below you can forward as-is. Thank you
> either way!
>
> <Your name>
>
> ---
> **Forwardable blurb:**
> <paste blurb from section B>

## B. The forwardable blurb (what the investor reads)

Short, vouched-for, one ask. The connector forwards this verbatim.

> <Your name> is the founder of <Company> — <one-liner: what you do for whom>.
> They're raising a <$X> <stage> round.
>
> Why I thought of you: <Fund>'s work with <PortfolioCo> / your focus on
> <space> lines up closely. The traction so far: <strongest 1–2 true proof
> points — e.g. $X MRR growing Y% MoM, N customers incl. <notable>>.
>
> They'd love 20 minutes to share what they're building. Deck here: <link>.

(~85 words. No attachment — a link only.)

## C. Your reply once the investor responds (connector → bcc)

When the investor replies, respond same-day and move your connector to bcc.

> Hi <Investor>, great to meet — and thanks <Connector> for the intro (moving
> you to bcc).
>
> <One sentence recap of the fit.> Would <day/time> or <day/time> work for a
> 20-minute call this week or next? Happy to send the data room ahead of time.
>
> <Your name>

## D. Closing the loop with your connector

A day or two after the meeting, regardless of outcome:

> Hi <Connector> — thank you again for the intro to <Investor>. <Quick honest
> update: we're doing a follow-up / it wasn't the right stage for them / they're
> reviewing the deck.> Really appreciate you spending some of your credibility on
> us — I owe you one. Anything I can help you with?

## Variations

- **Intro via a portfolio founder** (highest trust): the blurb can be even more
  casual; the founder's word that "this is a real, sharp team" carries the load.
- **Intro from an advisor/angel already on your cap table**: have them add one
  line of personal conviction ("I put in $X because…") — it does a lot of work.
- **If the connector offers to intro you to several investors**: provide one
  tight blurb plus a short ranked list, and let them choose; don't assume.

## Etiquette rules baked in

- Double opt-in always — the connector asks the investor before forwarding.
- Make the "no" easy in your ask to the connector.
- One investor per ask unless they offer more.
- Move the connector to bcc after the intro lands.
- Close the loop, win or lose. Gratitude is what keeps the network open.
- Never overstate the relationship or the traction.
