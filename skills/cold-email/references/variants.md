# Variants by stage and audience

Same skeleton (hook → what → traction → ask), different emphasis. Use these as
starting frames, then personalize the hook per investor. All examples use
**placeholder** numbers — replace with the founder's real figures, verbatim.

## By stage

### Pre-seed (lead with founder + insight)
At pre-seed there's little traction, so the hook carries more weight: the
insight and why *you* are the one to act on it.

> Subject: Pre-seed — <category> for <customer>
>
> Hi <Name>, your bet on <PortfolioCo> tells me you get <space>. I spent <N>
> years as <relevant role> and kept hitting <problem>; we're building <one-
> liner>. Early signal: <waitlist of N / 3 design partners / signed LOI with
> <notable>>. Raising a <$X> pre-seed. Open to a quick call?

### Seed (lead with traction + the wedge)
> Subject: Seed — <metric> in <timeframe>
>
> Hi <Name>, you led <PortfolioCo>'s seed — we're the <X> for <Y>. <$X MRR,
> Y% MoM, N customers incl. <notable>>. Raising <$X> seed, looking for a lead
> who's strong on <GTM/space>. Worth 20 minutes?

### Series A (lead with repeatable traction + warm path)
Cold converts worst at A; prefer a warm intro. If cold, lead with the metrics a
Series A investor underwrites (growth, retention, efficient GTM).
> Subject: Series A — <$X ARR>, <Y%> net retention
>
> Hi <Name>, <Fund>'s work with <PortfolioCo> on <motion> maps closely to what's
> working for us: <$X ARR growing Y%, Z% NDR, payback under N months>. Raising a
> <$X> A. Could share the data room — open to a call?

## By audience

### VC partner (firm-fit framing)
Tie to the firm's thesis/portfolio; show you know what stage and check they
write. One clear ask: a call.

### Operator angel (peer framing)
Angels back people and move fast. Lead with the insight and a specific reason
*they* are useful (they built in your space, they know your buyer). The ask can
be lighter: "would value 15 minutes / would love you on the cap table."

### Accelerator (program-fit framing)
Reference the program's focus and batch; show why you'd benefit from *their*
specific network/curriculum, not just money. Many have formal applications —
the email is to get noticed, not to replace the application.

### Strategic / corporate investor (mutual-value framing)
Lead with the strategic overlap and proof of pull, but keep it founder-first;
don't read as if you only want a partnership. Flag any sensitivities (info
rights, ROFR) for later, not in the first email.

## Personalization hook bank (fill with researched truth)

- "You led/backed <company>'s <round> — we're the <adjacent wedge>."
- "Your <post/podcast/thesis> on <topic> is the problem we solve."
- "We're seeing exactly the <pattern> you wrote about, for <customer>."
- "<Mutual contact / shared community> suggested you'd get what we're building."
  (only if true — and if there's a real mutual, use the `warm-intro` skill.)

## Reminder

Every example above is a *frame*. The hook line must be rewritten per investor
with something specific and verified. Re-check the fact (their recent deal, their
current focus) right before sending — these go stale fast.
