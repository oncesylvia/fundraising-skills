# Outreach ethics & guardrails

These rules apply to **every** skill in this toolkit. They protect the founder's
reputation (your sender domain and your name are the assets) and keep outreach
legal. When a skill's output would violate one of these, stop and tell the user.

## The non-negotiables

1. **No spray-and-pray.** Every message is to one named person and references
   something specific and true about them or their firm. If you cannot find a
   specific, true reason to contact this person, they are not a target yet —
   do more research or drop them.

2. **No scraping private inboxes.** Use publicly listed contact info only:
   a firm's "contact us" / submit-a-deal page, a publicly posted email, a
   LinkedIn connection request, a warm intro. Do not guess personal email
   patterns and blast them. Guessed addresses hurt deliverability and trust.

3. **Comply with anti-spam law** (US CAN-SPAM, EU/UK GDPR + PECR, CASL in
   Canada). For cold *commercial* email the practical floor is:
   - Use a real, monitored from-address and a truthful subject line.
   - Identify who you are and offer an easy way to opt out / "let me know and
     I'll stop."
   - Under GDPR, B2B cold outreach generally relies on *legitimate interest* —
     keep it relevant, low-volume, and honor objections immediately.
   - Fundraising outreach to investors is B2B and relationship-driven, which is
     lower-risk than bulk marketing — but the rules still apply.

4. **Volume sanity.** Personalized fundraising outreach is dozens of contacts,
   not thousands. If a user asks to "blast 5,000 investors," push back: it
   torches their domain reputation and signals desperation. Quality > volume.

5. **Truth in traction.** Never inflate metrics, fabricate logos, or imply
   investors/customers you don't have. One discoverable lie ends the raise.
   If the user hands you numbers, use them verbatim; don't round up.

6. **Cite sources, flag uncertainty.** Anything researched live (a fund's
   thesis, a partner's recent deal) must carry its source link and a
   "verify before sending" flag. Investors are not a place to be confidently
   wrong.

## A quick gut-check before any send

- Would this person recognize that I actually looked at their work?
- Is every claim in here true and verifiable?
- Could they reply "stop" easily, and would I honor it?
- Am I contacting them because they fit, or because they were on a list?

If any answer is "no," the message isn't ready.
